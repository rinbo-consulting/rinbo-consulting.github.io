Visualising UK electoral data: open source data, open source tools

Oct 12, 2014

The recent by-election in Clacton piqued my interest in next year’s general election, and I wondered how easy it might be to put together some visualisations showing what happened last time, and what might be happen in 2015.

1. Find the data

My first port of call was http://data.gov.uk, an initiative launched by the UK government in 2009 to enable easier access to data produced by the British public sector. I soon found on the Electoral Commission’s website, which had an Excel spreadsheet containing all of the 2010 results. A good start, and easy enough to get hold of.

2. Find the tools

The data’s arranged by constituency, so a geographic visualisation seemed appropriate. This gives me the excuse to have a play around with D3.js (Data Driven Documents), a great-looking JavaScript library for “for manipulating documents based on data”. The gallery of examples shows just what is possible.

3. How to do this?

I just wanted a simple one-page website to start with, but I wanted it to look presentable without too much effort. To this end, I used Bower to set up the components I intended to use.
